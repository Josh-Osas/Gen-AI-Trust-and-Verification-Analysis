# Do People Trust AI Too Much?
### A Data Story on Trust, Verification, and Generative AI

Generative AI is increasingly used for everyday decision-making.

But an important question remains:

**Do users actually understand when to trust AI — and when to verify it?**

This project explores how **education level, prompt type, and topic sensitivity influence user trust and verification behavior when interacting with AI systems.**

---

Trust in AI is not random.

It is influenced by three major factors:

- **User education level**
- **Type of question asked**
- **Risk level of the topic**

Understanding these patterns helps reveal **how people interact with AI and where blind trust may occur.**

---

# 1️⃣ How Does Education Influence Trust in AI?

![Education vs AI Trust](https://github.com/Josh-Osas/Gen-AI-Trust-and-Verification-Analysis/blob/main/chart%201.png)

One interesting pattern emerges when we look at education levels.

Users with **bachelor's degrees show the highest trust in AI responses**, while users with **master's degrees appear significantly more cautious.**

This suggests that **higher education may increase skepticism toward AI outputs rather than blind trust.**

Education may encourage users to question information sources — including AI.

---

# 2️⃣ Do More Educated Users Verify AI Results More?

![Education vs Verification](https://github.com/Josh-Osas/Gen-AI-Trust-and-Verification-Analysis/blob/main/chart%202.png)

Verification behaviour reveals an even stronger pattern.

Users with **PhDs spend the most time verifying AI responses**, followed by professionals and master's degree holders.

Users with only **high school education verify the least**, suggesting a higher risk of **blind trust in AI-generated information.**

This highlights the importance of **AI literacy and critical evaluation skills.**

---

# 3️⃣ Can AI Answer Every Prompt Equally Well?

![AI Accuracy by Prompt Type](https://github.com/Josh-Osas/Gen-AI-Trust-and-Verification-Analysis/blob/main/chart%203.png)

AI performance varies significantly depending on the **type of prompt**.

AI performs best in tasks that have **clear rules and objective answers**, such as:

- Math calculations
- Technical coding

However, accuracy drops when dealing with **subjective or opinion-based prompts.**

This means that **not all AI responses should be trusted equally.**

---

# 4️⃣ Which AI Answers Do Users Check the Most?

![Verification by Topic](https://github.com/Josh-Osas/Gen-AI-Trust-and-Verification-Analysis/blob/main/chart%204.png)

Interestingly, users spend the most time verifying AI responses in **high-stakes domains**.

The most verified topics include:

- **Legal advice**
- **Medical advice**

These are areas where mistakes could have serious consequences.

In contrast, users spend far less time verifying responses related to:

- Recipes
- General knowledge
- Creative prompts

This suggests that **users instinctively apply more caution in higher-risk scenarios.**

---

# Key Insights

From this analysis, three major insights emerge:

### 1️⃣ Education increases skepticism

Higher-educated users are more likely to verify AI responses.


### 2️⃣ AI performs best in structured tasks

Tasks with clear answers (math and coding) produce the most reliable outputs.


### 3️⃣ Users verify more in high-risk situations

Legal and medical responses are checked the most due to potential consequences.


# The Real Risk: Blind Trust

The biggest challenge with generative AI is not just accuracy.

It is **how users interpret and verify the information they receive.**

As AI becomes more integrated into daily life, improving **AI literacy and critical thinking** will be essential to ensure responsible AI use.

The future of AI isn't just about **better algorithms**.

It's also about **smarter users.**
